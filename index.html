Major Introduction

The Korean Language Education and Korean Culture Major aims to cultivate professionals equipped with a balanced competence in Korean language education theory and instructional design for teaching Korean as a foreign language, along with humanistic knowledge of both traditional and contemporary Korean culture.

Year 1

Academic Korean: Speaking, Reading, Writing, Presentation
Generative AI Literacy

Year 2

Introduction to Korean Linguistics
Korean Orthology
Understanding Korean Literature
Contrastive Linguistics
Studies in Korean Grammar

Year 3

Second Language Acquisition
Pronunciation Teaching
Grammar Teaching
Comprehension Teaching
Assessment
Materials Development

Year 4

Teaching Practicum
Contemporary Popular Culture Analysis
Narrative Analysis

Educational Objectives

Education Professionals: Develop education professionals with classroom management skills and competencies in curriculum design, assessment, and materials development, grounded in Korean linguistics, Korean language education, and Korean studies.
Cultural Understanding: Foster critical understanding of the historical and contemporary dimensions of Korean cultureâ€”from traditional heritage to modern popular cultureâ€”and build skills in content analysis and storytelling.
Digital Literacy: Establish digital literacy, including appropriate use of generative AI, as an auxiliary means to enhance teaching-learning effectiveness and academic communication in evolving educational environments.
Global Career Support: Strengthen intercultural communication and practical skills for multicultural and multilingual settings to support career advancement in domestic and international educational institutions and cultural content industries.
